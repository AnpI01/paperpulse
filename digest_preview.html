9 OmniStream: Mastering Perception, Reconstruction and Action in Continuous Streams

Yibin Yan, Jilan Xu, Shangzhe Di et al. • arXiv

OmniStream unifies perception, reconstruction, and action into a single visual backbone for versatile, real-time understanding.

OmniStream introduces a unified visual backbone designed for real-time streaming environments, enabling perception, reconstruction, and action from diverse visual inputs. It employs causal spatiotemporal attention and 3D positional embeddings for efficient online processing via a persistent KV-cache. Pre-trained on 29 datasets, OmniStream demonstrates competitive performance across various vision tasks, including robotic manipulation, by generalizing across semantic, spatial, and temporal reasoning.

9 DVD: Deterministic Video Depth Estimation with Generative Priors

Hongfei Zhang, Harold Haodong Chen, Chenfei Liao et al. • arXiv

DVD deterministically adapts video diffusion models for SOTA depth estimation, using 163x less task-specific data.

This paper introduces DVD, a novel framework that transforms pre-trained video diffusion models into deterministic single-pass depth regressors, addressing a long-standing trade-off in video depth estimation. It tackles issues like stochastic hallucinations from generative models and the extensive data needs of discriminative models. DVD achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot performance and unlocks geometric priors from video foundation models using significantly less task-specific data.

8 BiGain: Unified Token Compression for Joint Generation and Classification

Jiacheng Liu, Shengkun Tang, Jiacheng Cui et al. • arXiv

BiGain unifies diffusion model token compression, boosting classification and maintaining generation quality via frequency-aware operators.

BiGain is a novel, training-free framework that accelerates diffusion models by optimizing token compression for both image generation quality and classification accuracy. It achieves this by using frequency-aware operators, like Laplacian-gated token merging, to disentangle fine details from global semantics during compression. This approach consistently improves the speed-accuracy trade-off for diffusion-based classification while maintaining or enhancing generation fidelity, supporting lower-cost deployment.

8 EVATok: Adaptive Length Video Tokenization for Efficient Visual Autoregressive Generation

Tianwei Xiong, Jun Hao Liew, Zilong Huang et al. • arXiv

EVATok adaptively tokenizes videos, significantly boosting quality and efficiency for autoregressive video generation.

This paper introduces EVATok, a framework for adaptive video tokenization designed to improve autoregressive video generative models. It addresses the inefficiency of uniform token assignment by dynamically allocating tokens based on video complexity, preventing wasted tokens on static segments while better representing dynamic ones. EVATok achieves superior reconstruction and state-of-the-art class-to-video generation with significant token savings compared to prior methods.

9 Attend Before Attention: Efficient and Scalable Video Understanding via Autoregressive Gazing

Baifeng Shi, Stephanie Fu, Long Lian et al. • arXiv

AutoGaze dramatically accelerates and scales MLLMs for high-resolution video understanding by intelligently pruning visual redundancy.

This research introduces AutoGaze, a lightweight module designed to enhance the efficiency of Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) in processing long, high-resolution videos. By autoregressively selecting a minimal set of multi-scale patches, AutoGaze significantly reduces visual token redundancy. This approach enables MLLMs to process much longer and higher-resolution videos faster and more effectively, improving performance on video understanding benchmarks and a new long-form video QA benchmark, HLVid.

8 EndoCoT: Scaling Endogenous Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in Diffusion Models

Xuanlang Dai, Yujie Zhou, Long Xing et al. • arXiv

EndoCoT enables iterative MLLM chain-of-thought reasoning in diffusion models for complex, step-by-step task execution.

This paper introduces EndoCoT, a novel framework addressing limitations in integrating Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) with diffusion models for complex tasks. It improves reasoning depth by iteratively refining MLLM latent thought states and bridging them to the diffusion model's denoising process. This allows for progressive, step-by-step execution of complex instructions, significantly outperforming baselines on diverse benchmarks like Maze and Sudoku.

8 SciMDR: Benchmarking and Advancing Scientific Multimodal Document Reasoning

Ziyu Chen, Yilun Zhao, Chengye Wang et al. • arXiv

SciMDR's framework and large dataset significantly advance multimodal reasoning for scientific documents.

This paper introduces SciMDR, a novel framework and large-scale dataset designed to improve multimodal document reasoning in scientific contexts. It employs a two-stage pipeline, "synthesize-and-reground," to generate faithful QA pairs and re-embed them into full-document tasks, addressing challenges in dataset scale and realism. Models fine-tuned on SciMDR demonstrate significant performance gains on scientific QA benchmarks, particularly for complex document-level reasoning.

8 MM-CondChain: A Programmatically Verified Benchmark for Visually Grounded Deep Compositional Reasoning

Haozhan Shen, Shilin Yan, Hongwei Xue et al. • arXiv

MM-CondChain reveals MLLMs significantly struggle with deep, visually grounded compositional reasoning across diverse domains.

This paper introduces MM-CondChain, a novel benchmark designed to evaluate Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) on their ability to perform deep, visually grounded compositional reasoning, particularly in complex workflow scenarios. The benchmark features multi-layer reasoning chains, where each step requires detailed visual perception and sequential reasoning over compositional conditions. Experiments show that even state-of-the-art MLLMs significantly struggle with these tasks, indicating a fundamental gap in their capabilities.

8 GRADE: Benchmarking Discipline-Informed Reasoning in Image Editing

Mingxin Liu, Ziqian Fan, Zhaokai Wang et al. • arXiv

GRADE benchmark reveals significant limitations in multimodal models for discipline-informed image editing reasoning.

This paper introduces GRADE, the first benchmark designed to assess discipline-informed reasoning in image editing for unified multimodal models. It evaluates models on their ability to perform image edits under structured, domain-specific constraints across 10 academic fields. Experiments reveal substantial limitations and large performance gaps in current state-of-the-art models when tackling these knowledge-intensive editing tasks.

8 Separable neural architectures as a primitive for unified predictive and generative intelligence

Reza T. Batley, Apurba Sarker, Rajib Mostakim et al. • arXiv

Separable Neural Architectures offer a unified, domain-agnostic framework for predictive and generative intelligence by leveraging factorisable structure.

This paper introduces Separable Neural Architectures (SNAs), a new representational class that unifies additive, quadratic, and tensor-decomposed neural models by explicitly leveraging factorisable structure. SNAs impose structural inductive biases, enabling distributional modeling of chaotic systems while also applying to discrete sequences. Demonstrated across diverse domains, SNAs offer a versatile, domain-agnostic primitive for unified predictive and generative AI.
